Colter Wehmeier

An interaction designer and researcher exploring digital experiences through games, spatial computing, and community engagement.

Dual-PhD candidate bridging Informatics and 20th-century cultural heritage at UIUC and the Cyprus Institute. I develop interactive software that transforms scholarly virtual environments into participatory research platforms—spaces where public memory, stories, and insights shape our understanding of architectural heritage.

My work leverages Building Information Modeling (BIM) and Virtual Environments to advance the Architectural Digital Humanities, grounded in Stiegler's vision of mnemotechnical systems that merge social practices with technological frameworks for collective memory.

Supported by the Fiddler Innovation Fellowship at NCSA, advancing computational approaches for humanities research and public engagement.
